# Assumption Surfacing

Surface hidden assumptions before research, planning, or scoping begins.

## When to Use
Anytime you're about to make a big plan and want to pressure-test your thinking first. Roadmap planning, feature scoping, go-to-market strategy, research design — ten minutes of this catches assumptions you didn't know you were making.

## Process

1. **Identify the topic.** Ask the user what decision, initiative, or question they're exploring.

2. **Read any briefing materials.** If project docs exist, read them to establish what's actually known vs. assumed.

3. **Run the AUQ interview.** Ask 6-8 questions in 2-3 batches covering:
   - Target definition (who is this for?)
   - Key requirements (what do they need?)
   - Business model implications (pricing, timeline, GTM)
   - Competitive context
   - Internal assumptions about feasibility

   Use multiple-choice options to make responses concrete, but always include an "Other" escape hatch.

4. **Map answers to KNOW vs. ASSUME.** For each answer:
   - **KNOW:** Supported by briefing docs, data, or direct evidence
   - **ASSUME:** Came from the user's mental model, unsupported by evidence

   Present this as a clear table with the user's specific answers quoted.

5. **Convert assumptions to research questions.** Each assumption becomes a testable question the research needs to answer — not confirm.

6. **Save to `assumptions.md`** in the project folder with columns: Assumption, Source, Confidence (high/medium/low), and Research Question.

## Key Principle
The goal is awareness, not judgment. Every PM carries assumptions — the dangerous ones are the ones you don't realize you're carrying. This process makes them visible so you can test them deliberately instead of confirming them accidentally.
