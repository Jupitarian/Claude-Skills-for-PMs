# Research Plan

Compile assumptions, instruments, and decision criteria into a stakeholder-ready research plan.

## When to Use
When you've already done the thinking — surfaced assumptions, designed instruments — and need to compile everything into a clean artifact a stakeholder could read and approve.

## Reference
See `research-plan-template.md` in this skills folder for the document structure.

## Process

1. **Gather inputs.** Read existing project files:
   - Assumptions (from assumption surfacing or ad hoc)
   - Interview guide (if exists)
   - Survey (if exists)
   - Any briefing docs or context

2. **Compile the research plan** using this structure:
   - **Context** — Why this research exists, what triggered it
   - **Research questions** — Mapped from assumptions, specific and testable
   - **Assumptions table** — Each assumption with source, confidence, how to test, risk if wrong
   - **Methodology** — Interview and/or survey design, who, how many, timeline, sequencing
   - **Success criteria** — "We'd know the answer if..." statements that are concrete and falsifiable
   - **Decision framework** — "If research shows X, then we do Y" for positive, mixed, and negative signals

3. **Save to `research-plan.md`** in the project folder.

## Key Principle
The decision framework is the most important section. Most research plans tell you what you're going to learn but not what decisions you'll make based on what you find. Define what "good enough" data looks like and what you'll do if you don't get it — otherwise you'll rationalize whatever the data says into the answer you already wanted.
