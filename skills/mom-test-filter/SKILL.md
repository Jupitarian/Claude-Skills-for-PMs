# Mom Test Filter

Review any set of interview questions against Mom Test principles and flag violations.

## When to Use
Before any customer research — interviews, user testing, discovery calls. Hand it a draft interview guide and it flags every question that would get polite lies instead of truth. Works as a quality gate before any conversation with customers or prospects.

## Reference
See `mom-test-reference.md` in this skills folder for the full framework.

## The Three Principles

1. **Past behavior over future hypotheticals** — "Would you use X?" becomes "How do you handle X right now?"
2. **Their life over your idea** — "What if we built Y?" becomes "Walk me through your last procurement cycle"
3. **Specifics over generics** — "Is security important?" becomes "Tell me about the last time a security concern blocked a purchase"

## Process

1. **Read the interview guide or question list.**

2. **Scan each question against the three principles.** Flag any question that:
   - Asks the interviewee to predict future behavior ("Would you...", "Could you see yourself...")
   - Describes your idea/product before asking the question
   - Uses leading framing ("Don't you think...", "Wouldn't it be better if...")
   - Asks for generic opinions instead of specific stories ("Is X important?")
   - Asks about hypothetical willingness to pay

3. **For each flagged question:**
   - Name which principle it violates
   - Explain why it would produce unreliable data
   - Provide a rewritten version that follows Mom Test principles

4. **Apply fixes to the file.**

## Key Principle
The unifying move: redirect from prediction to memory, from your idea to their reality, from abstract to concrete. If someone can answer your question with a polite lie, rewrite it.

## Note on Surveys
The Mom Test applies specifically to conversations (interviews, discovery calls). Surveys have their own bias taxonomy — leading preambles, double-barreled questions, hypothetical pricing. Related but distinct. Don't force survey questions through the Mom Test framework; use standard survey bias checks instead.
