# Research Design

Full research design pipeline — from vague strategic prompt to stakeholder-ready plan.

## When to Use
When you're starting a research effort from scratch. Runs the full process: assumption surfacing, question design with Mom Test filter, instrument creation, and plan compilation.

## Process

### Step 1: Assumption Surfacing
- Read any briefing materials (emails, meeting notes, strategy docs)
- Run the AUQ interview (6-8 questions in 2-3 batches) to surface the user's mental model
- Map each answer to KNOW vs. ASSUME
- Convert assumptions to testable research questions
- Save to `assumptions.md`

### Step 2: Research Plan Skeleton
- Define research questions from assumptions
- Draft decision framework ("if we learn X, we do Y")
- Draft success criteria ("we'd know the answer if...")
- This becomes the spine — instruments serve the plan, not the other way around

### Step 3: Interview Guide Design
- Write 1-2 questions per assumption, organized by theme
- Map every question to the assumption it tests
- Run Mom Test filter on all questions:
  - Past behavior over future hypotheticals
  - Their life over your idea
  - Specifics over generics
- Fix any violations
- Save to `interview-guide.md`

### Step 4: Survey Design
- Write questions to quantify patterns across a broader sample
- Run survey bias checks (distinct from Mom Test):
  - No leading preambles
  - No double-barreled questions
  - No hypothetical pricing
  - Include "N/A" and "I don't know" options
- Fix any violations
- Save to `enterprise-survey.md` (or appropriate name)

### Step 5: Compile Research Plan
- Combine all artifacts into a single stakeholder-ready plan
- Include: context, research questions, assumptions table, methodology, success criteria, decision framework
- Save to `research-plan.md`

## Key Principles
- The plan is the spine. Build it first, then design instruments to serve it.
- Every question has a job. If you can't map a question to an assumption, cut it.
- Mom Test is for conversations. Surveys get their own bias filter.
- The decision framework matters most. Define what you'll DO with the answers before you collect them.
